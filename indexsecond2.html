Main Cast

In James Cameron’s Titanic (1997), the most memorable child character is Cora Cartmell, an eight-year-old steerage passenger who dances with Jack at the Irish party. She was played by Alexandrea Owens-Sarno, who won the role in an open casting call when she was in third grade. During filming, she and her younger sister spent six months on location, and Leo DiCaprio famously befriended her—bringing peanut butter-and-jelly sandwiches and playing games between takes

Kate Elizabeth Winslet (born 5 October 1975) is an English actress who rose to international fame playing 17-year-old Rose DeWitt Bukater in James Cameron’s Titanic (1997). Rose is introduced as a wealthy, idealistic socialite engaged to Cal Hockley, but feeling trapped by her upper-class life aboard the ill-fated ship. Her chance meeting with Jack Dawson sparks a brief, passionate romance that defines the film’s central love story. Winslet’s performance earned widespread acclaim and cemented her status as a leading actress; she was nominated for several awards (including a BAFTA and a Golden Globe) and became synonymous with the character of Rose
